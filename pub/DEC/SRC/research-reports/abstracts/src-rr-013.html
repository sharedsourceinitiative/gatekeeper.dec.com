SRC Research Report 13

Retiming Synchronous Circuitry.

Charles E. Leiserson and James B. Saxe.

August 20, 1986
42 pages

This paper shows how the technique of retiming can be used to transform a given synchronous circuit into a more efficient circuit under a variety of different cost criteria. We model a circuit as a graph, and we give an O(|V| |E| lg |V|) algorithm for determining an equivalent circuit with the smallest possible clock period. We show that the problem of determining an equivalent retimed circuit with minimum state (total number of registers) is polynomial-time solvable. This result yields a polynomial-time optimal solution to the problem of pipelining combinational circuitry with minimum register cost. We also give a characterization of optimal retiming based on an efficiently solvable mixed-integer linear programming problem.
